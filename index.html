Santa's Snack

A tradition centuries in the making! Since the Victorian era, Londoners have left sweet mince pies and a warming glass of sherry for Santa, with a carrot added for Rudolph since the 1940s to help him fly through the city fog.

Mince pies are Britain's favourite sweet snack for Christmas. the pastry is filled with fruit, spice, and citrus. The first pies were rectangular with baby Jesus on top, made with pastry. The pies are made with 13 ingredients, representing Jesus' disciples, which is symbolic of the Christmas story. Dried fruits, such as raisins, prunes, and figs, as well as lamb and mutton, were among the ingredients used to represent shepherds. Spices like cinnamon, cloves, and nutmeg were also included to represent the wise men. For good luck, stir in a clockwise direction, always make a wish when eating your first pie of the Christmas season, and never cut a pie with a knife.
